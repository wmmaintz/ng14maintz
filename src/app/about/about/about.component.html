This website is meant to share the many accomplishments of those who share my last name, Maintz. I think that all of us, at one time or another, have wondered about our family tree. Have you ever "Googled" your last name to see how many hits it receives?

With a last name like Maintz, it's a little easier to track one's ancestry than it would be with a surname like Smith, for instance.  My grandparents were German immigrants who settled in eastern Pennsylvania during the depression. My paternal grandfather was a machinist in the Bethlehem Steel Mill and my maternal grandfather was a Dixie-Cup factory worker. My father joined the U.S.Air Force and made a career of if, so we moved around a lot. Every 3 years we were moving somewhere new. One tour of duty took us to Upper Heyford, England. That gave us the opportunity of vacationing in various parts of Europe each summer. It was quite an education and it gave me a chance to meet some of my distant relatives. Since then, I've discovered a number of distant relatives who've now settled in other parts of Germany, the Netherlands, and other parts of Europe. And I've also found a number of Maintz's here in various parts of the United States. In fact, one member of the Maintz clan settled in a town just south of St. Louis, Missouri and was generous enough to donate a number of acres of land as a home for all species of birds. Information about the Maintz Wildlife Preserve, around the town of Cape Girardeau, Missouri can be accessed via the Links page).

According to ancestry.com, as of January 2014, there are 1.8 million members on record within the Maintz family tree (see  registry).

I've tried to capture some of the websites and user profiles of other Maintz's on the internet and have placed links to them on the Links page. The list continues to grow so please don't think that this is an exhaustive list. Please feel free to search on your own.
